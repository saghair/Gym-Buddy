Training guides

Short, practical guides to help you train safely and stay consistent.

How to warm up properly

Basics

A good warm-up should make you feel more awake, slightly warm, and ready to move, without leaving you tired before your workout.

Start with 3–5 minutes of light cardio such as walking, cycling, or marching in place. Follow this with dynamic movements that copy your training session: leg swings, arm circles, easy bodyweight squats, and gentle torso rotations.

Avoid long, static stretches before heavy lifting. Save them for the end of your session when your muscles are warm and relaxed.

Choosing the right program

Planning

The best program is the one you can follow consistently. It should match your schedule, your level, and your main goal.

Beginners usually do well with full-body workouts 2–3 times per week. If you already have some experience, you might enjoy a split like upper / lower or push / pull / legs.

If your life is very busy, choose a simple routine and protect those few training days instead of aiming for a “perfect” plan you never finish.

Basic gym etiquette

Gym life

Wipe down equipment after use, put weights back where they belong, and avoid blocking mirrors or machines when you are resting.

Do not sit on a machine while scrolling your phone if someone is clearly waiting. Offer to let others work in with you if you are doing many sets.

Everyone starts somewhere. Respect others, focus on yourself, and use headphones if you like your music loud.
